EAP Guidance — based on this week's data
Your team's stress signal is elevated. Here's what you can do as a manager.
1
Start with a group check-in
Open your next team meeting with "How is everyone feeling this week?" — no pressure to share, just normalise the conversation. Avoid singling anyone out.
2
Make EAP visible, not personal
Mention EAP as a team resource — not directed at anyone. Try: "Just a reminder that our EAP is free, confidential, and available to everyone."
3
If someone seems to be struggling
Ask privately: "I've noticed you seem a bit off lately — is there anything I can support you with?" Then listen. You don't need to fix it.

Leadership this week
💡 This week's focus
Psychological Safety
When stress is high, employees need to feel safe to speak up. One simple move: next time someone raises a problem, respond with curiosity before solutions. Try "Tell me more about that" before jumping to fix mode.
Based on Amy Edmondson's research on team psychological safety
3 things to try this week
○ Acknowledge the team's effort publicly in your next standup
○ Block 15 min for a 1:1 with someone who seems quiet lately
○ Share one thing you personally found challenging this week — model vulnerability
